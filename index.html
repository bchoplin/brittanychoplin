Hi. I'm Brittany.

If there were a movie about my career, the AOL dial-up sound would play over the opening credits and the scene would open with a preteen girl huddled over a clunky computer in the early aughts. Always curious about technology, I discovered the joy of coding the way many millennials did… by experimenting with an ever-evolving Internet.

My GeoCities site introduced me to the exhilaration of creating something out of nothing. I bought my first domain name when I was 12, after begging my parents for it the way many that age would ask for a new bicycle. My very first clients were friends looking to spruce up their Xanga and MySpace pages. They each compensated me with a secure position in their Top 8.

Many years later, I am a front-end engineer with over a decade of professional experience who strives to deliver a wonderful experience to all users. My technical expertise is in front-end languages. I have extensive knowledge of accessibility, performance optimization, front-end architecture, technical debt reduction, and testing methodologies. My interest in developer experience has grown after witnessing peers grappling with legacy codebases.

Amongst colleagues, I am best known for my clear and direct communication, high standards of quality, and commitment to continuous improvement for myself, other individuals, and the team. As a versatile and fast learner, I constantly seek to deepen my knowledge and understand the “why.” I am comfortable flexing to full-stack engineering and have experience in technical product management and leading engineering teams. I value skills that go far beyond the CLI and IDE.

Outside of work, I find myself drawn to finding and fostering community. I manage a book club with over 600 Facebook members and facilitate in-person monthly meetings with 30+ attendees. I volunteer at a local yoga studio in exchange for classes. I am also a member of a women’s running club.

I am currently seeking new opportunities and would love to connect for leads, networking, or just a friendly chat.
